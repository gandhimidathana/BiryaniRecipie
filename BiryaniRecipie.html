Biryani Chronicles – My Most Loved Recipe! 📖

Aromatic, flavorful, and rich—this biryani recipe is a perfect blend of spices, tender meat, and fragrant rice, bringing the ultimate taste of tradition to your plate!"

Ingredients

  • 2 cups Basmati rice (long-grain)
  • 4 cups water
  • 2-3 bay leaves
  • 2 green cardamoms
  • 1 black cardamom (optional)
  • 1-inch cinnamon stick
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon ghee or oil
  • 500g chicken/mutton (bone-in preferred)
  • 1 cup thick yogurt (curd)
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper powder
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced (for frying)
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/2 cup milk (optional)
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • 2 tablespoons chopped coriander leaves
  • 2 tablespoons chopped mint leaves
  • A pinch of saffron strands (or food color)
  • 2 tablespoons ghee
  • 1/4 cup fried onions
  • 2 tablespoons chopped coriander & mint leaves

Instructions

  1. Wash & Soak Rice:
    • Rinse basmati rice in water until clear.
    • Soak it for 30 minutes, then drain.
  2. Cook Rice:
    • Boil 4 cups of water in a pot, add rice, bay leaves, cloves, cardamoms, cinnamon, shah jeera, salt, and ghee.
    • Cook until 70-80% done, then drain and set aside.
  3. Marinate Chicken:
    • In a bowl, mix meat, yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric, coriander powder, garam masala, black pepper, salt, lemon juice, and oil.
    • Cover and marinate for 1 hour (or overnight for best results).
  4. Prepare Masala:
    • Heat ghee/oil in a deep pan.
    • Fry sliced onions until golden brown. Remove some for garnishing.
    • Add tomatoes, red chili powder, cumin powder, garam masala, salt, and green chilies.
    • Cook until oil separates.
  5. Cook the Meat:
    • Add the marinated meat to the masala.
    • Cook on medium heat until tender (chicken: ~15 min, mutton: ~40 min).
    • Add milk (optional) for richness.
  6. Layer the Biryani:
    • In a heavy-bottom pot, spread a layer of cooked meat.
    • Add a layer of half-cooked rice.
    • Sprinkle saffron milk, fried onions, coriander, and mint leaves.
    • Repeat layers until all rice and meat are used.
    • Drizzle ghee on top.
  7. Dum Cooking (Steaming):
    • Cover tightly with a lid and cook on low flame for 20-25 minutes.
    • (Tip: Seal the lid with dough for authentic dum cooking.)
  8. Serve:
    • Gently mix the layers before serving.
    • Enjoy hot with raita, salan, or a side of your choice!
